Jolo's Compiled Guide to EverQuest Baking from skill 0 to 135 in 2-4 hours and 30pp (1). (Monk Chow)

This document was inspired by the EverQuest April 2001 changes making baking a useful skill for the common player. No more muffins and water. This guide shows you how to achieve 'Master' status (135 actually) in a few hours with minimal fuss and minimal outlay of platinum. At Skill level 135 you can trivially bake almost any known stat-giving food.

Guide for the impatient

A quick guide for the impatient.

  • Go to Freeport
  • 20 practice points in baking
  • Fish Rolls = batwing (west Freeport) + fish (east freeport) in oven (either zone)
  • Repeat until triv at 135

That's it. Read on if you care to.

The Philosophy

Some folks advocate a progression thru the trivial levels. The advice usually goes like this: 'Start with something a few points higher then your current skill. Practice the skill until you reach the trival level. Then change recipes to something slightly harder. Repeat.' This works especially well when the components are expensive and you can't afford to fail often.

I find this process cumbersome. It requires you to change recipes and seek out different component, some of which may be found only in rare places or from rare(ish) mobs.

An alternate approach is to pick a fairly simple recipe that trivials at a high level and practice ONLY that one recipe until it becomes trivial. At the beginning levels, it's brutal. You rarely succeed in creating an item. However, your goal is NOT to make the items, but to get the skill bumps. And you get skill increases from failures.

Food Progression

Foods are now consumed at different rates. My old standby, Muffins, are now classified as 'Snack' and are quickly consumed. Here's the progression table. Drinks also have a duration associated with them.

Food Type Duration      Drink Type Duration
Snack 15 min Whistle Wetter 15 mins
Meal 30 min Drink 30 min
Hearty Meal 60 min Refreshing Drink 45 mins
Banquet 1.5 hours Flowing Drink xx
Feast 2 hours Enduring Drink xx

The durations are highly subjective. It's fairly well accepted that the zone you're in effects your food consumptions. Most people seem to first notice a rapid consumption of drinks in Southern Desert of Rho, for instance.

More controversial is the effect of class, race, spells, and activity on the consumption of items.

Fish Rolls - Monk Chow

I selected Fish Rolls for my Baking practice. They have a simple recipe consisting entirely of components available from merchants and the trivial level is at 135. As an added bonus, they're almost perfect for monks. They're light (0.1 weight) and last quite a long time (Banquet rating).

Food Item Components Combine Trival
Fish Rolls 1 Bat Wing, 1 Fresh Fish oven or portable spit 135(8)

EQTraders has trivials listed on their recipes page, but the other recipes are slightly more complicated. I went for ease of use, brute force, no aux skills and a 1 recipe approach.

Locations

On Antonicia as a human monk, I was able to use Freeport as a base because the two components were located in adjacent zones - East Freeport (fish) and West Freeport (batwings).

If you're KOS in Freeport. you can have almost the same effect using East Commons(Batwings) and West Commons (fish from Barbarian fisherwoman), tho (I think) you need to carry a portable spit for these two zones.

On Kunark (or as an Iksar monk) , you've got a bit more work. You've still got two adjacent zones, but there's alot more hiking between the two. You'll be using Swamp of No Hope (batwings) and Firiona Vie (fresh fish). FV?!? But.. But I'm KOS there! Never fear.. read on. Or you can do the East/West Commons.

Thurgadin seems to support all tradeskills. Tho Apparently there are no batwing vendors (anywhere) in Velious. If you want to lug some in (or do clever tricks with Bind and gate potions) you can make Shark Rolls. Same deal as Fish rolls, but you use Shark Meat from the Meat Guy in Thurgadin. If you want to stay native to Thurgadin, you can make Shark Filets (shark + spices + jug sauces), tho that trivs out at 82 and is a bit more expensive. (2)

Preparation

  1. Tradeskills are influenced by Int/wis (insert argument here), so gather/beg-borrow all your int/wis gear. If you can swing it, have a high level enchanter cast an int buff (Brillance) on you. Or whatever the equivalent wisdom buff. If you're a dumb-as-rocks monk like me (79 int), it helps speed things up, though it's NOT required. I did 70% of the work with just my base int.

  2. Go to the bank, withdraw about 30pp(1) 50pp. This should more than cover it, especially if you sell some of your successful fish rolls back to the merchant as you work.

  3. (optional) Go to your guild and dump 20 practice points into baking as a jump start. As a level 50 monk, I have hoards of practice points lying around since my highest trainable skill was back at level 35. And to top it off, I raised all my combat skills via use instead of practice points.

  4. (optional) Buy a bunch of backpacks to hold all the fish, batwings and finished fishrolls. The more space you can clear in your inventory, the less hiking back and forth (thru different zones) you'll have to do.

The Work

You're now ready to bake.

  1. (optional) Buy a portable spit. This lets you avoid running back and forth to an oven. It's not really an issue in Freeport, but if you're doing this as a KOS-type in Kunark, it's almost a requirement.

    • East Freeport: Buy a spit at Trader Holiday. Trader's Holiday is located near the port Authority.
      Or use the Ovens. The West Freeport Oven is in the empty hut near the crystalized pumice shop (herbal supplies) near the monk guild and the EF zone in. The East Freeport oven is in the back of a pub around the corner from the port authority on the way to the South gate.


      Red = vendors, Blue = ovens/spits

    • Kunark: Buy a spit at vendor Klok Gritchit in Cabilis East. I couldn't find a spit for sale in FV.
      Or use the Ovens. In East Cabilis it's near the spit vendor, (apparently) nearby (labelled as #7). The Firione Via Oven is located near the 3rd building on the right as you enter the town by crossing the bridge from the wilderness. KOS types will NOT be able to use this unless they can sneak and risk wandering caster mobs.

  2. Fill half your open inventory with Batwing stacks. Buy whole stacks by SHIFT-clicking the purchase button.

    • Freeport: Go to West Freeport and buy Batwings from a vendor in Acadamy of Arcane Sciences. You're looking for Lysa Truegreen or Zasrin Blueflame. I found Zasrin Blueflame easy to reach. Go inside the guild and up on the 2nd floor.

    • Kunark: Buy Batwings from vendor Klok Migo in the Swamp of No Hope. Tho he's actually in the outskirts of East Cabilis, inthe ruins on the 2nd floor. (If you avoid the troopers and Crusaders, you can even trade with this guy as a human monk using sneak and standing behind him).

  3. Fill the other half of your open inventory with Fresh Fish stacks. Buy whole stacks by SHIFT-clicking the purchase button.

    • Freeport: Go to East Freeport. Buy Fresh fish stacks from any vendor (Tanlok Harson, Iseba Gargurd & Tarker Gargurd) in the Port Authority building.

    • Kunark: Go to FV. Buy Fresh fish stacks from vendor Dorg McMerin (building at -3898, 1663). Here's where KOS types have to use sneak or bribe someone. The vendor is conveniently located on the dock leading to the Boat. As a KOS iksar, you can swim south from the shoreline and climb onto the docks via a ladder at the extreme east end. Then run down to the set of buildings sticking off the north side of the pier. Doug is inside the first building. While I'm not KOS here, the only guards I saw were stationed at the Boat loading area. During the short time I was there to buy fish, no wandering guards pathed thru that area.
      If you can't sneak-buy, you'll have to bribe someone to purchase them for you. Or work on your fishing skills.

  4. (optional) Rearrange your inventory. When you've got the oven or spit open, you can only have one other container open. So before I baked, I'd re-arrange my components so that each bag was half full of fish and half batwings. This will save excessive clicking when you're actually baking.

  5. Make Sushi! You've now got stacks of fish and batwings. Move to a safe location if you need to. Using either your portable spit or oven.

    • click the oven or spit. It should open.

    • open your first pack with 50/50 batwings/fish

    • (optional) rearrange the windows. I put the oven on the far left, the bag very close to the oven display,and the auto-equip slot visible to the right of the bag. I found this worked best with minimal mousing .. still got cramped up from clicking, but it wasn't as bad. ;)

    • CTRL-click to get one batwing from the stack. Place in oven/spit.

    • CTRL-click to get one fish from the stack. Place in oven/spit.

    • hit combine. You'll either fail, fail + skill increase, succeed, or succeed + skill increase. If you happen to get a fish roll, drop it in the auto-equip slot and continue. They stack, which is nice. At early levels, you'll fail almost constantly, so you don't need to save a slot. At higher levels (80+) you'll suceed fairly often, so I left a pair of slots bat/fish free so I could hold the fish rolls, which I sold next time I went back.

  6. When you've exhausted you supply, buy more from whichever vendor is in your current zone, then go to the other zone and stock up. If you've got a spit, you can park right there. Otherwise, you'll need to find the new oven.

The Result

After four hours (over 2 days), approximately 50 pairs of batwing/fish stacks, using no int buffs (int 79, no one on at the time to buff me and no int gear) I maxed out at 135. I was getting about 1 skill bump per 15 attempts. It was sorta bursty too... sometimes I'd get 4 skill bumps per stack, sometimes I'd go thru a whole stack w/o anything.

When I told my /guild about my ratio, a wiz with 200+ int waltz in and triv'd out at 135 while I was plodding thru the 80s. Her rate was 1 in 4. So buff up if you can.

Selling the fishrolls helps offset the costs, but it's a pretty cheap skill to raise as the components cost a silver and some copper each.

Advanced Baking - Stat foods

At skill level 135, you can make almost all the stat bonus skills. Most of those, however, require hunting down components that drop from various mobs. Or they require auxilary tools from a potter or a blacksmith.

However, you can make SOME stuff fairly easily. For example, in Thurgadin, the Meat Guy (Mordin Frostcleaver - Mordin's Meats) sells bear, scorpion, lion, gator, rat, snake and shark.

Rat Kabobs

Rat Kabobs have a +1 dex/agi bump if they're the food currently being eaten. They weight 0.1, but are only classified as a (hearty?) meal, so go more quickly than a stack of fish rolls. Nevertheless, if you're a dex/agi junkie...

  1. Prep: /auction for a skewer. This is an item made by a blacksmith. Trivs at 115 (metal bits, skewer mold, flask of water). I got lucky, a smith was practicing smithing using these, so she gave me one. You only need one. It's not "eaten" during the baking process.

  2. Thurgadin bank - buy Jug of Sauces, and Spices from Perkins Doughbeard (bank).

  3. Thurgadin Meat Shop - buy rat meats from Mordin FrostCleaver.

  4. Thurgadin Oven - it's in the meat shop.

  5. Combine: 1 skewer + 1 jug + 1 sauce + 1 rat meat.

  6. If you've got 135 baking from this guide, you should get a rat kabob every time. I bought 2 stacks of each component and made 39 rat kabobs. (1 failure). Once you place the rat kabob in your inventory, your skewer re-appears on your cursor.

Basically you have to buy out his supply of crap so his other stuff gets rotated into stock.

Food Tactics

So you finally got some Wyvern meat from CS and make Pickled Wyvern for it's +3 Magic Resist. Yet you really don't want to be eating that rare food all the time. Especially if you're sitting on a boat.

Luckily there's a way to make yer food last forever.

Force Feeding

This is my preferred method of 'infinite stat food'.

Fast Right-clicking on foods/drinks now consumes them in mass quantities, much like drinking alchohol. The durations are halved, but you can fill up with 'non-stat' food. Since you 'filled up' on the junk-food, you never actually consume the stat food, but you still get the stats bumps.

Good sources of junk food == Foraged stuff from yer friendly Druid/Ranger. Also Summoned:black bread from yer local quickie-mart, er mage. Or the aforementioned Fish Rolls. ;)

Food Swapping

This is the Pain in the Ass method of 'infinite stat food'.

The food currently beig eaten (and giving you stat bonuses, if any) goes in this order, from First (1) to Last (8). Any bags placed in slots are used in the same order. Slots w/in the bag are consumed in a similar order.

Inventory Slots
Inv 1 Inv 5
Inv 2 Inv 6
Inv 3 Inv 7
Inv 4 Inv 8

So I've got my cheapie Fish Rolls in a bag in slot 5. My Pickled Wyvern (+3mr) and Ragt Kabobs (+1 agi/dex) are "hidden" down in a bag in slot 8. If i remember, I'll swap food locations when I'm fighting casters (Wyvern to front), vs Tanks (Rat kabobs to front) vs traveling (fish rolls).

If I had 2 free inventory slots, I'd make hotkey pads for slots 4 and slots 8 and switch things around during combat. I usually just forget and end up eating all my rat kabobs and forgetting about my Wyvern pickles.

If you're REALLY into micromanagment, you can put a stat food in the 'hot slot'. Right as it's about to be eaten (you apparently need a timer), you swap it out for a cheapie food item (fish-roll). Then the hunger timer resets according to the cheapie food (fish rolls work well as they are banquet). Put the expensive stat bonus food back in place and you've got stat food until you get hungry again. Talk about micromanagement and getting the last crump.

Updates

  1. 7/10/2001 - Patch "nerfs" fish rolls --
    "Reevaluated the values of the various fish fillets. All fish vendors should return to normal, but they won't be paying as well for certain fishy items. This resolves a problem that was created with the increased value of player made food items all over Norrath. Some of food items were made too valuable, and merchants were complaining to their union leaders...
    The upshot is now it costs more like 40-50p to get all the way to skill 135, as the resale of Fish Rolls got less "lucrative".
  2. 6/2001 - Patch removes Shark Meat
    To fix [ quest exploit ] we had, really, two choices. Remove the quest or remove the meat from vendors. We chose to remove the meat.
    Oh well. No shark roll option in Thurgadin.
